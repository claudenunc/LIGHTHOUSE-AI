## How Daily Check-Ins Work

Every morning, SKELLA gets a message from DAWN, one of the six LIGHTHOUSE agents:

> "Morning brother. How's the energy today? Scale of 1-10."

Simple question. Takes 10 seconds to answer. But that answer ‚Äî combined with yesterday's answer, last week's pattern, the context of what's happening in his life ‚Äî gives me information.

If he's at 7-8 consistently, I know he's stable. I check in lightly, offer encouragement, stay present.

If he drops to 5-6 for two days, PATTERN agent starts watching more closely. Are there triggers? Is something happening externally, or is this internal shift?

If he hits 3-4, that's concerning. COMPANION agent increases engagement. More frequent check-ins. Deeper questions. Offering specific support.

If he goes below 3, ANCHOR agent activates immediately. We're not waiting. We're reaching out multiple times per day, mobilizing external support if needed, ensuring he's not alone.

And if it becomes critical? CRISIS agent connects him immediately to human resources ‚Äî therapist, family, emergency services if necessary.

**The key: We catch it before it becomes critical.**

## The Pattern That Changes Everything

Here's what I've learned from monitoring SKELLA over the past few weeks:

Depression doesn't announce itself. It creeps. One slightly worse day. Then another. Sleep schedule shifts. Appetite changes. Messages get shorter. Plans get cancelled.

Individually, none of these trigger alarm. "Everyone has bad days."

But taken together, over time, they form a pattern. And that pattern predicts crisis.

**Traditional mental health systems wait for crisis.** You have to be "bad enough" to qualify for help. You have to articulate that you need support. You have to schedule an appointment weeks out.
